## Post 2

- **Post link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kashmala-malik_i-pasted-my-gsc-data-into-claude-and-it-exposed-activity-7455545385034539008-a3Uq?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADbafy8BLR7CG_of6vm1lvATyLHBuQYWtMg
- **Topic:** AI citation cannibalization
- **Key idea:** Kashmala explains that she pasted her GSC data into Claude and discovered AI citation cannibalization. She says this happens when multiple pages partially answer the same query, but none answer it completely. As a result, AI engines may cite a competitor’s single definitive page instead. Her fix involves using GSC data, asking Claude to find clusters where multiple pages compete for the same intent, choosing one page to own the query, consolidating content, writing a 60-word answer block, adding FAQ schema, redirecting weak pages, and checking citations in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini.
- **Why it matters for AI-powered SEO content production:** This is directly relevant because it shows how AI can support content consolidation, content gap detection, answer block creation, schema planning, and citation optimization. It also shows that AI-powered SEO content production is not only about creating more pages, but about making one strong page that fully answers the query and is easier for AI engines to cite.
